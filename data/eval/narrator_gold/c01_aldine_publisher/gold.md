The collection holds **15 books from the Aldine Press**, the celebrated Venetian house founded by Aldus Manutius. Because Aldine imprints appear under many Latin and Italian forms, the matching drew on several of the press's real imprint statements as they survive in the records — *aldine press, venice*, *apud Aldi filios*, *apud Aldum*, *in aedibus Aldi*, *in aedibus Aldi et Andreae soceri*, and *in casa de' figlivoli di aldo* among them — so the set spans the founder, his heirs, and the Asolani partnership.

As one would expect of the Aldine programme, these are overwhelmingly editions of the classics. The earliest is a **1501 *Juvenalis, Persius*** — from the very first years of Aldus's octavo classics — alongside **Suetonius, *XII Caesares* (1516)**, **Pliny the Younger's *Epistolarum libri X* (1518)**, the lives of *Nerva, Trajan and Hadrian* drawn from Dio (1519), and a Greek **commentary of Joannes Grammaticus on Aristotle's *Posterior Analytics* (1534)**. The vernacular is represented too, by Cicero's *Epistole famigliari* "tradotte di nuovo" (1559). Every imprint is Venetian, and the languages run from Latin and Greek to Italian.

Several of the classical authors gathered here — **Cicero, Juvenal, Persius, Suetonius** — carry linked Wikipedia, Wikidata, VIAF and NLI authority records in the catalogue, and each title resolves to its entry in the TAU Primo catalogue for consultation.

The Aldine list in this collection is a compact but representative window onto the press whose italic type and pocket-format classics reshaped European reading in the sixteenth century.
