The collection holds **10 books illustrated by Ludwig Schwerin**, all of them German-language volumes printed in Leipzig in the 1920s and early 1930s. Schwerin (linked in the catalogue to his Wikipedia, Wikidata, VIAF and NLI authority records) worked as an illustrator in the rich world of Weimar-era book art, and these records capture that activity directly through his *illustrator* role.

The set ranges across literature and art writing. It includes Franz Landsberger's *Impressionismus und Expressionismus: eine Einführung* (1920); Rainer Maria Rilke's *Briefe an eine junge Frau*; a children's picture book, *Es war einmal* (1923), after Ludwig Richter; the song collection *Alte und neue Lieder* (193x); D. H. Lawrence's *Der Fuchs* in German translation (1926); and Stefan Zweig's legend *Die Augen des ewigen Bruders* (1933). Each is linked to its TAU Primo catalogue entry.

It is a coherent little corpus: a single illustrator, one printing centre, and a tight window of roughly fifteen years, documenting Schwerin's contribution to German fine-printing before his emigration.
